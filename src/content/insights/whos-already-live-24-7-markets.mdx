Traditional markets close. Onchain markets don't — and the biggest names in trading are now building for that reality. **Coinbase's $2.9 billion acquisition of Deribit** and **Robinhood's tokenized stocks** are the clearest signs that 24/7, continuously settling markets are moving from crypto-native venues into the mainstream of finance.

**The short version:** always-on markets and tokenized equities are being built by major institutions, not just DeFi protocols. Here's what's shipped and the question it raises.

## Who's already doing it

**Coinbase — building the "everything exchange."** Coinbase [completed its roughly $2.9 billion acquisition of Deribit](https://www.theblock.co/post/366957/coinbase-completes-2-9-billion-cash-and-stock-acquisition-of-deribit), the world's largest crypto options platform (over $1 trillion in 2024 volume), to bring spot, futures, perpetuals, and options into one venue. It is rolling out [24/7 trading of BTC and ETH contracts and plans tokenized stocks](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/coinbase-launches-stock-trading-but-tokenized-stocks-are-the-real-ambition/), pending regulatory approval — explicitly building a market that never closes.

**Robinhood — tokenized equities, already live abroad.** Robinhood expanded **tokenized stock trading for European customers**, putting equity exposure onto blockchain rails for retail — a direct step toward always-on, tokenized markets for traditional assets.

**The onchain-native layer.** Beneath the household names, onchain perpetuals and derivatives venues already trade continuously, settling without a central clearinghouse — the model the larger players are now moving toward.

## What the onchain version actually changed

The core shift is the removal of the market-hours gap. Hedging, leverage, and price exposure become available 24/7, and trades settle continuously rather than waiting on clearing windows and business days. For derivatives, onchain perpetual markets run with continuous funding instead of fixed expiries. The result is a market that matches how global business actually operates — across time zones, without a 4 p.m. close.

One honest caveat: some of this is still gated by regulation. Coinbase's tokenized stocks await approval, and the rules for tokenized equities are still forming — context we cover in [Why now: what the GENIUS and CLARITY Acts mean for builders](/insights/why-now-genius-clarity-act).

## What this could mean for your business

If your business depends on market access, hedging, or trading, the baseline is shifting from "during market hours" to "whenever you need it." Treasuries that can hedge on a Sunday, products that can offer round-the-clock exposure, settlement that doesn't wait for Monday — these become possible, and eventually expected.

It won't matter to every business, and chasing 24/7 access for its own sake is a cost, not a strategy. But for firms where timing and continuous risk management matter, the always-on model is being built by the largest players right now.
