For years the standard objection to tokenization was "the serious institutions won't touch it." That objection is gone. **BlackRock, Franklin Templeton, and JPMorgan** — three of the most risk-averse names in asset management — now run live tokenized funds. When they move, it is rarely a bet; it is a read on where distribution is going.

**The short version:** tokenization has crossed from concept to product at the top of the market. Here's who's live, what it changed, and the question it raises for you.

## Who's already doing it

**BlackRock — the flagship is onchain.** BlackRock's tokenized U.S. Treasury fund, **BUIDL** (issued via Securitize), holds [roughly $2.3 billion in assets and runs across nine blockchains](https://www.mexc.com/news/1087467) including Ethereum, Solana, Polygon, Arbitrum, Avalanche, and Aptos. In May 2026 BlackRock [filed two more tokenized funds with the SEC](https://thenextweb.com/news/jpmorgan-tokenized-fund-ethereum-genius-act), including a digital share class for a multi-billion-dollar money-market fund. The world's largest asset manager is expanding onchain, not testing it.

**Franklin Templeton — tokenized and regulated.** Franklin Templeton, an early mover, runs a **tokenized U.S. dollar money-market fund** with regulated distribution — extending a traditional, conservative product onto public blockchain rails.

**JPMorgan — tokenizing the hard stuff.** JPMorgan tokenizes funds on its **Kinexys** platform, including [a private-equity fund for select clients](https://thenextweb.com/news/jpmorgan-tokenized-fund-ethereum-genius-act) and an onchain net-yield fund launched in late 2025 — taking on the harder, less-liquid asset classes where tokenization's benefits are largest.

## What the onchain version actually changed

Tokenizing a fund turns a share into a programmable token: it can settle near-instantly, move 24/7, carry its compliance rules in code, and reach new distribution channels — while every holding is transparent on-chain. For illiquid assets like private equity or credit, it also opens the door to fractional ownership and secondary liquidity that the traditional structure can't offer. The fundamentals are covered in [What is RWA tokenization?](/insights/what-is-rwa-tokenization).

## What this could mean for your business

If BlackRock and JPMorgan are tokenizing Treasuries, money-market funds, and private equity, the question for any business that *holds, issues, or distributes assets* is simply: which of ours is a candidate, and is now the time? Funds, credit, real estate, commodities, loyalty assets — each has a different answer.

And the honest answer is sometimes "not yet" — tokenizing an asset with no distribution or liquidity advantage just adds cost. The value is in picking the right asset and the right moment. The managers moving now are doing it deliberately, not reflexively. If you want to know whether one of your assets belongs in that category, that's the conversation we have.
