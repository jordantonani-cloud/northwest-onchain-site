Private credit is one of the most conservative corners of finance — which is exactly why its move onchain matters. When **Apollo** and **Janus Henderson** put real credit funds on public blockchains, with **S&P** rating them and Wall Street infrastructure forming around them, "DeFi yield" becomes "institutional credit, onchain."

**The short version:** the biggest credit names are already live, and the plumbing — ratings, redemptions, infrastructure — is maturing fast. Here's who's doing it and what it should make you ask.

## Who's already doing it

**Apollo — a $785B manager, onchain.** Apollo offers crypto-native and institutional investors blockchain-based access to one of its private-credit strategies — the tokenized **ACRED** fund, issued through **Securitize** — and [the experiment drew more than $100 million within months of launching](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-21/apollo-s-private-credit-trade-finds-new-audience-on-blockchain). For one of the world's largest alternative managers, that's a deliberate distribution move, not a science project.

**Janus Henderson — a $1B onchain CLO fund.** Janus Henderson launched **JAAA**, a fully onchain AAA CLO fund [seeded with $1 billion and built on Centrifuge](https://centrifuge.io/blog/centrifuge-janus-henderson-grove-tokenized-aaa-clo-fund). Its tokenized Treasury fund (JTRSY) earned the [highest tokenized-fund rating from S&P](https://centrifuge.io/blog/janus-henderson-anemoy-rating) — a traditional rating agency validating an onchain product.

**The infrastructure is institutional now.** Centrifuge provides the rails for Apollo, Janus Henderson, and S&P Dow Jones Indices, with around $1.66 billion in total value locked. And a [new $1 billion liquidity facility now gives tokenized funds like BlackRock's BUIDL and Janus Henderson's JTRSY instant redemptions](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/05/14/blackrock-janus-henderson-tokenized-funds-get-instant-redemptions-with-new-usd1-billion-facility) — closing one of the last gaps between onchain and traditional funds.

## What the onchain version actually changed

For the issuer, distribution and servicing become software, reaching pools of capital a traditional fund can't easily touch. For the investor, the collateral and cash flows are transparent and verifiable in real time, with features like in-kind redemptions and instant liquidity. Tokenized private credit is now the largest non-Treasury segment of the roughly $34 billion RWA market — we explain the mechanics in [Onchain credit and vaults: new product lines explained](/insights/onchain-credit-and-vaults-explained).

The transparency is the point — but it cuts both ways: onchain doesn't fix a bad loan book, it just makes the risk visible. That's a feature for serious allocators, not a bug.

## What this could mean for your business

If you issue credit, onchain distribution is becoming a way to reach new capital with less servicing overhead and more transparency than the traditional structure. If you allocate to credit, onchain funds increasingly offer visibility — into collateral, reserves, and redemptions — that opaque private vehicles can't match. Either way, the bar set by Apollo and Janus Henderson is now the reference point.

The right move depends entirely on your assets, your investors, and your risk appetite — and sometimes the answer is to wait. But the firms treating this seriously now are the ones shaping how their corner of credit goes onchain. If that's a question on your desk, it's the conversation we have.
